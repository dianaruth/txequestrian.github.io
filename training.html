Idylwood Stables

The Texas Equestrian Team trains with coach Sydney Kornman at Idylwood Stables. The stable is located about 20 minutes from campus.


Lessons

Team members wishing to compete must participate in at least one lesson each week with the team coach. Beginner riders will ride in a private lesson until they are able to join a group. Group lessons will be about an hour and a half long with same skill level riders. During many group lessons, riders will switch horses multiple times to best simulate the IHSA competition format. Lessons may include work on the longe line, riding without stirrups, a variety of equitation exercises and tests, gymnastic jumping and course riding.


Workouts

Riding is a sport and as such, participants must be physically fit and prepared to perform their best. The team has several members who are very experienced in fitness and can offer advice on exercises including cardio conditioning, core strength training, balance evaluation and training, and leg and upper body strength exercises. Workouts can be arranged with these members to improve riding strength as well as overall fitness.


The Stables

Facilities include a large sand arena for flat work and a lighted arena with jumps, a large grassy area with field jumps, and covered grooming and tacking areas. More information about Idylwood Stables can be found at www.idylwoodstables.com.


The Horses

The team rides the Idylwood lesson horses during lessons. Lesson horses have a wide range of sizes and capabilities. All of the horses are show quality, kind and safe. All of our horses are patient, hard-working teachers and we are thrilled to be able to learn with them!

Coach Bio

Sydney Kornman has essentially been in the saddle since birth. Like her mother and rancher grandfather before her, she started out Western, showing 4-H, but fell in love with eventing during her time in Canada. Pony Club manuals still sit on the shelf of her old house in Nova Scotia and she brought those tenets, along with the name Idylwood Stables, to Texas. She believes devoutly that every rider should be well-versed in horsemanship and dispenses with grooms and barn hands to teach hands on management of horses. "My goal," she says, "is that when a person 'graduates' from my barn, they should be able to go out and buy any horse and care for it on their own with minimal vet/farrier assistance."

Her passion for the graceful performance of equitation began with Jen Hamilton, whom George Morris called "the best equitation rider in North America." Her own training techniques are firmly rooted in classical dressage and modern equitation. "It's like ice skating and ice hockey," she quips. "I like the skating. I don't want to see the effort in the ride."

Sydney's love for UT and college life stems from her 30-year relationship with the university community; her husband was a UT professor until his death in 1999. She holds three degrees from the University of Texas and is a life member of the Texas Exes. She is thrilled to be at the helm of the team.

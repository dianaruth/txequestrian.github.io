Membership Requirements

  • Currently enrolled UT student; to compete at shows, members must be enrolled in at least 12 hours.
  • Pay all IHSA and Team Dues
  • Maintain a 2.25 GPA or higher
  • Provide documentation of health insurance (required by IHSA and UT)
  • Meet lesson, practice, team criteria to compete

Costs of Membership

Team Dues - $70/semester

IHSA Dues - $30/semester

USHJA Fee - $15/year

Lessons - $170/month or $500/semester

Show Fees - $30/class, $45/weekend trainer fee, meal money


Where does the money go?

  • Team Dues go directly to the team. This money is used to pay for travel expenses for shows, socials, and other team expenses
  • IHSA Dues go to IHSA Nationals, Zones, and Regional Shows and expenses
  • Lesson fees go directly to the Coach
  • As a student organization, the team does not receive any financial support from the university. All expenses are paid for by way of donations and fundraising
  • We can receive additional funding from the University through active participation
  • The University DOES NOT pay for any services rendered by the Team coach, nor does it pay for the use of the horses or facility

Time Commitment

The time commitment for the team is a perfect balance between work and play, providing team members with several opportunities to ride, while still maintaining plenty of time for their studies.


Lessons

Team members are divided into lesson groups, taking a one hour-long lesson a week with the Coach. Beginner riders may need to take 30-minute private lessons before joining a group. If the Coach must cancel a lesson due to an emergency or unsafe weather conditions, the lesson will be rescheduled for a later date in the semester. If a rider misses their lesson, they are responsible to make up this lesson on their own. Make-up lesson weeks are provided at various times during the year. Open lesson spots due to rider cancellations can also be taken by a rider who needs to make up a lesson.
